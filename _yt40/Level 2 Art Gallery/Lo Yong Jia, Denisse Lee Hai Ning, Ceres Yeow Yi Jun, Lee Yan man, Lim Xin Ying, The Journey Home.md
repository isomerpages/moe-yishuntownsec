<h4>Lo Yong Jia, Denisse Lee Hai Ning, Ceres Yeow Yi Jun, Lee Yan man, Lim Xin Ying, <strong><em>The Journey Home</em></strong>, Mixed media on canvas</h4>
<p>This artwork advocates for environmental care by highlighting the impact
of human waste on marine life. Bubble wrap, broken glass, and potato sacks
symbolise beach pollution, surrounding a turtle forced to swim through
debris. It urges viewers to reflect on their actions and adopt more mindful,
sustainable habits. t calls for greater awareness and responsibility, urging
viewers to reconsider their relationship with nature and to protect the
environment for future generations through mindful and sustainable choices.</p>
<p><strong><u>See and Wonder</u></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>How would the turtles survive if their beach continues to be covered in
human waste?</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>What does the contrast between the clean ocean and polluted shore tell
us about human responsibility?</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Does this artwork remind you of any real-life places where nature is affected
by pollution?</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><u>Activity:</u></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Imagine you are the turtle returning to a polluted shore. Write its thoughts
and feelings in about 20 words.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Design a poster to raise awareness about pollution and to encourage humans
to protect the environment.</p>
</li>
</ul>
