<h4>Chee Baoping, <strong><em>Family Time</em>, </strong>oil on canvas</h4>
<p>This artwork reflects the quiet shift in family time within a technology-driven
world. Though physically together, each figure is absorbed by their own
screens, revealing emotional distance. It questions how digital distractions
reshape connections, urging viewers to reconsider presence, attention,
and the meaning of togetherness today.</p>
<p><strong><u>See and Wonder:</u></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Look at the foreground figure on the right. An eye is detached and a heart
is attached to the phone. Is the phone a part of her body or is it a parasite?</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>On the left panel, a family photo is pixelated. What do you think the
artist is saying about our digital memories?</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><u>Activity:</u></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Design a “Phone Jail” for your family to use during meal times. Write
down one question you would ask your family members that <em>cannot </em>be
answered by a Google search.</p>
</li>
</ul>
